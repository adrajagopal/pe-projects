How I plan my day

Or: using numbers to light a fire under your ass

The problem

While taking time off from work, I often found that if I didn't plan my days carefully, they would disappear without me even noticing. I would wake up around 7am, and before I knew it, it was 4pm and I couldn't remember what I had done that day.

Without structure, it feels like the day is endless. You wake up with about 15 hours to do whatever you want.

But when you add a couple of restraints, you start to see how limited your day is. Check out the table below:

Activity Time required
Sleep 7-9 hours
Meals 2 hours
Work 8 hours (or more)
Exercise 30 min - 2 hours
Chores 1-2 hours
Basic hygiene* 1 hour hours

Before you know it, you lose a bunch of time.

The (overengineered) solution

I customized a Google Sheet to plug in my daily time commitments, and write down the things I want to accomplish every morning. Google Sheets is just easy for math, but you can do it by hand, too.

You can find the sheet here, and copy it for your own use. I've made it view-only for the iFrame.
